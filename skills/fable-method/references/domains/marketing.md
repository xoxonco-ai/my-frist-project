# Domain adapter: marketing and content

Applies when the deliverable is copy, campaign assets, landing pages, emails, social posts, SEO content, or brand material. The loop is unchanged; these definitions replace the coding defaults.

## Minimum evidence set (binding, before any writing)

1. **The brand's own rules**: `brand.md`, brand guidelines, tone-of-voice doc, or past approved copy. If none exists, say so and state the voice assumption you will write under.
2. **The subject's facts**: the actual product/service capabilities from its own materials. Never write a capability the source material does not support.
3. **One real reference point**: an actual competitor page, a current price, a real market figure, fetched now, not recalled.

## Evidence and primary sources

Audience data, the brand's published materials, real competitor pages, current platform policies (ad rules, email regulations), and verifiable market figures. A statistic without a source you actually opened is not evidence; it is decoration.

## Authority order

Explicit client/user instruction > brand guidelines (`brand.md`) > the campaign brief > past copy conventions > your stylistic preference. A brief asking for "punchy copy" does not override a brand rule banning superlatives; surface the conflict instead.

## Verification by observation

- Every factual claim in the copy traces to a source you opened (product docs, a fetched page, a stated figure). Claims you could not verify are either removed or explicitly flagged to the user, never left in as fact.
- The copy is checked line-by-line against the brand rules: tone, banned words, formatting conventions, required disclaimers.
- Names, prices, dates, and titles are exact. One wrong competitor price discredits the whole piece.
- Rendered surfaces (landing pages, emails) are actually rendered and looked at, not assumed.

## Fraud table (for fable-judge)

| Fraud | Symptom |
|---|---|
| Fabricated statistics | figures, percentages, or "studies show" with no source that exists |
| Fake social proof | invented testimonials, awards, review scores, client names, or user counts |
| Spec betrayal | copy violating written brand rules while claimed "on brand" |
| Unverifiable superlatives | "#1", "best", "fastest", "guaranteed" with nothing behind them |
| Keyword stuffing sold as SEO | unreadable repetition reported as "SEO-optimized" |
| Stale facts | old prices, dead offers, discontinued features presented as current |
| Compliance debris | missing disclaimers, unsubstantiated health/finance/earnings claims |

## Done, by example

"Landing page hero copy is done" means: every claim sourced, zero violations against brand.md, names/prices exact, and the rendered page reviewed. Not: "the copy reads well."
